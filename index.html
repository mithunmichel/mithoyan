I'm Mithun Murali — a UX designer who joined straight from IIT Bombay's IDC School of Design and quickly found myself operating well beyond a junior brief. Today I lead design across two live products in one of India's most technically demanding domains: drone intelligence and geospatial data.

My work isn't consumer-facing. I design for GIS analysts, defence operators, surveillance engineers, and government institutions — people whose workflows have no margin for error. That means deep research, tight collaboration with engineering, and the ability to translate complex technical realities into interfaces that feel effortless.

I've owned end-to-end product design — from stakeholder workshops and customer discovery to rapid Figma prototyping and final delivery. I've presented POCs directly to enterprise clients, shaped business decisions, and mentored junior designers and interns along the way.

Away from screens, I draw. Always have. Pencil sketches, Procreate illustrations, caricatures — the observational instinct that feeds my design thinking.

Flyght Cloud

End-to-end UX ownership of a cloud-based drone data processing platform — serving GIS professionals, defence operators, and government institutions across three distinct product modules.

Role
Lead UX Designer
Scope
End-to-End Product
Verticals
GIS · Surveillance · Government
Model
B2B SaaS · Drone-Agnostic

The Platform

Flyght Cloud is a drone-agnostic, cloud-based data processing platform serving the GIS and surveillance industries. Any drone, any operator — upload data, extract intelligence. The platform is structured across three modules, each serving a distinct user need and industry vertical.

Map Module
Video Module
Audit Module ⟨NDA⟩

Map Module — GIS & Geospatial Analytics

The Map Module is the core of Flyght Cloud for GIS professionals. It handles the full pipeline from raw drone imagery to processed geospatial deliverables — orthomosaics, DEMs, DTMs, DSMs — and layers advanced analytics on top for measurement, comparison, and reporting across industries.

Designing this meant understanding the GIS analyst's mental model deeply: they think in layers, projections, and coordinate systems. The interface had to feel native to that world while remaining accessible to field operators who are not GIS specialists. I led the end-to-end design — from workflow architecture and information hierarchy to component-level interaction design.

Video Module — ISR & Surveillance

The Video Module is an ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance, Reconnaissance) product for defence and enterprise customers. It handles video ingestion, annotation, and analysis from aerial drone footage — a domain where latency, precision, and situational clarity are non-negotiable.

The design challenge here was handling high-information-density interfaces without overwhelming the operator. I developed a display logic system that surfaces critical data progressively, informed by rounds of testing with actual surveillance operators.

Audit Module — Government & Enterprise

⟨ NDA — Select process work shown ⟩ · Client: Central Government Institution, India · Work in progress

The Audit Module emerged from a strategic opportunity: digitising a large-scale government audit process that had been conducted entirely manually. I owned the problem definition, rapid prototyping, and customer presentation — working from rough sketches and flow diagrams to a high-fidelity Figma prototype across multiple rapid iterations.

The prototype was presented directly to the client institution and received strong validation, significantly accelerating the sales process. The process artefacts — flow diagrams, journey maps, iteration documentation — are available on request.

MapAssist

A precision desktop application bundled with military-grade and industrial UAVs — enabling operators to geotag, stitch, and pre-process drone imagery in the field before ingestion into Flyght Cloud.

Role
Solo UX Designer
Platform
Windows Desktop
Users
UAV Operators · Field Surveyors
Distribution
Hardware-Bundled · Not Retail

Context & Users

MapAssist is not a consumer product. It ships exclusively with IDEO Force's military-grade and large industrial UAVs — the kind used for defence mapping, infrastructure inspection, and precision agriculture at scale. The users are field operators and survey technicians: highly skilled, time-pressured, and operating in environments where a software error has real-world consequences.

The application handles image stitching and critical back-end pre-processing before drone data is transmitted to Flyght Cloud. It sits at the seam between hardware and cloud — a role that demands reliability, clarity, and zero ambiguity.

The Design Problem

Existing geotagging tools fell into two camps: developer-grade CLIs that required technical expertise, or bloated GIS plugins that buried the core workflow under irrelevant features. Neither was designed for the post-flight ritual of a field operator who needs to process data quickly and get back to mission-critical work.

I spent time in the field observing operator workflows. The post-landing sequence was consistent across teams: offload storage, cross-reference GPS logs, geotag images, validate outputs, export for processing. Every existing tool broke that flow in at least two places.

Design Decisions

MapAssist was designed around that natural workflow sequence — Import → Review → Geotag → Validate → Export — with the map always visible and errors surfaced immediately with clear resolution paths. No hidden settings. No mode-switching. The entire session history is accessible without losing your current position.

The desktop-first constraint was a genuine design opportunity: larger screen real estate, keyboard shortcuts, and the ability to show spatial data and metadata in parallel — things that would be impossible on a mobile-first interface.
